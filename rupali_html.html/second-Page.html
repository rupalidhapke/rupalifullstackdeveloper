TOP THREE PERSONOLITTIES

  • MAHATMA GANDHI

    Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi (2 October 1869 – 30 January 1948) was an Indian lawyer,
    anti-colonial nationalist and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance M
    to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule, and to
    later inspire movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.
    The honorific Mahātmā (Sanskrit: "great-souled", "venerable"), first applied to him
    in 1914 in South Africa, is now used throughout the world.
